A bit of History

Charleston is the oldest and second-largest city in the State of South Carolina. The city lies just south of the geographical midpoint of South Carolina's coastline and is located on Charleston Harbor, on the mouth of the Ashley and Cooper rivers. Founded in 1670 as Charles Towne in honor of King Charles II of England, Charleston adopted its present name in 1783. By 1690, Charles Towne was the fifth largest city in North America,and it remained among the ten largest cities in the United States through until about 1840.
Known for its rich history, well-preserved architecture, distinguished restaurants, and mannerly people, Charleston has received a large number of accolades, including "America's Most Friendly City" in 2013 by Cond√© Nast and also "the most polite and hospitable city in America" by Southern Living magazine.

Weather

Temperature
The month of March is characterised by rising daily high temperatures, with daily highs increasing from 19C to 22C over the course of the month. Being the South it is still relatively sticky and humid in March.
Sun Shine
The Sun in March generally rises at about 7am and sets at about 6.30pm. Its gererally sunny and you can get sunburnt if out and about all day. Whilst most locals and Australians may disagree it is generally warm enough for a trip to the beach in the middle of the day.

Money

South Carolina is part of the USA and so uses the US Dollar. Generally you can get Breakfast and Lunch for about $10 per person. Dinners depend on where you go to eat but typically set you back around $20-40 per person. Beers range from $3-5, some places have $1 bottle nights. Liquor drinks cost anywhere from $6 to $10 depending on the size and place.
